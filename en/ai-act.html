AI Act — it's not a problem. It's your argument.

There's a lot of talk about risks. Not much about the fact that clear rules are exactly what a serious brand needs to operate without fear. We've put together everything you need to know — and everything you can show your board.

What does the AI Act actually say about advertising?

EU Regulation 2024/1689 came into force on 1 August 2024. Full obligations for the advertising industry will apply from 2 August 2026. The rules apply to everyone who produces and distributes AI-assisted advertising content — agencies, production houses and brands directly.

  • Labelling — any video, audio or graphic generated by AI that could mislead viewers as to its authenticity must be clearly labelled. Not buried in the terms and conditions, not in the footer. Visible.
  • Prohibition of manipulation — subliminal techniques and targeting of psychological vulnerabilities (e.g. children, people in difficult situations) are absolutely prohibited. No professional agency does this — so no problem.
  • Prohibition of impersonation — if a voice or face in an ad is not real, the viewer must know.
  • Metadata and watermarks — AI tools (Midjourney, Runway, OpenAI) are legally required to embed hidden watermarks in files. These are not removed in post-production.

Cannes 2025. Award. Scandal. What does it mean for your brand?

In 2025, Brazilian agency DM9 received the Grand Prix in the Creative Data category for a campaign for the Consul brand. A few days later, Cannes Lions withdrew the award — all 12 trophies. Reason? Fabricated results. Fake testimonials. Doctored footage from CNN Brasil and a TED Talk, presented as proof of the campaign's effectiveness — it was just editing. The agency's chief creative officer resigned.

This wasn't a problem with AI. It was a problem with dishonesty — AI was simply the tool used to deceive the jury.

In 2026, Cannes Lions drew its conclusions. New rules: mandatory AI disclosure in every entry, sign-off from both CEO and CMO on the case study, content detection tools. Result? Submissions dropped by 25% — from 26,900 to 20,050. Organisers called it a success. Fewer entries, but honest ones.

And the Film Grand Prix for 2026? It went to Anthropic, with the campaign "Ads Are Coming to AI. But Not to Claude." A simple, witty, 100% traditional ad about the fact that the best AI model doesn't want to show ads. The jury rewarded the idea — not the technology.

Audiences and jurors don't reject AI. They reject mediocrity, dishonesty and the absence of an idea. If AI shortens the journey from strategy to screen — no one objects. If it's used to conceal the lack of a strategy — it shows. Every time.

Your board has questions? We have answers.

We work with companies whose boards have doubts. That's normal — and it's good that they ask. Here are the most common objections, with answers you can show them.

"Is AI in advertising legal?"
Yes. The AI Act does not ban AI in advertising. It regulates how it is used — transparency and ethics. Just as advertising law doesn't ban advertising — only dishonest advertising.
"Will we face legal problems if we use AI?"
No — if the material is properly labelled and doesn't use manipulative techniques. Production with AI House is compliant with the AI Act by design, because that's how our process is built. You receive not just a film — you also get a complete set of information and labelling materials for correct disclosure across every channel.
"Won't viewers reject the ad because it's AI?"
Research and festival juries say the same thing: viewers reject weak ads — regardless of how they were made. AI itself is not the issue. Quality is the issue. And that's what we're responsible for.
"Is an AI ad cheaper but worse?"
It's not worse — it's different. It's faster. And it allows you to produce things that traditional production would price many times higher — or simply couldn't deliver at all. We talk about projects, not about a cheaper version of something.
"Isn't it better to wait until the market stabilises?"
The market has already stabilised — in other companies. Brands that started with AI production a year ago now have an advantage in speed, cost and scale. Waiting today is a real cost — measured in campaigns your competition has already run.
